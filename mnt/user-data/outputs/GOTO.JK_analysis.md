## B. Fundamental Health (8/30 pts)

**Assessment: Transitional — The Turnaround Is Real But Incomplete**

**Bull case fundamentals (significant):**
- **Adjusted EBITDA turned positive:** Rp 393B (Q1 2025) → Rp 427B (Q2 2025). H1 2025 = Rp 820B. Full-year guidance Rp 1.4-1.6T.
- **Revenue acceleration:** +37% YoY (Q1), +23% YoY (Q2). Net revenue at ~IDR 17.9T for 2025F.
- **Fintech segment exploding:** Revenue +90% YoY in Q1 2025. Consumer loan portfolio +108% to Rp 5.7T → Rp 6.6T (Q2). Targeting Rp 8T loan book by Dec 2025. This is the hidden moat and profitability engine.
- **Core GTV surging:** +54% YoY (Q1), +43% YoY (Q2). The platform is gaining wallet share.
- **Cloud migration complete (Jun 2025):** Hosting costs cut ~50%. Direct margin expansion driver.
- **2026F net income breakeven/positive:** Analysts project +Rp 189B net income in 2026 — first-ever GAAP profit.
- **ROE forecast:** 9.3% within 3 years (vs. current negative).

**Bear case fundamentals (serious):**
- **Still GAAP loss-making:** 2025F net income est. = -Rp 1,632B. The forward P/E is meaningless (-10.3x).
- **No dividend:** Zero income return. Pure capital appreciation bet.
- **CEO change:** Patrick Walujo out, Hans Patuwo in (Dec 2025). Leadership transitions during turnarounds introduce execution risk.
- **Competitive intensity:** ODS segment GTV growth moderating as Grab and others compete aggressively.
- **Piotroski F-Score:** Estimated ~3-4/9 (improving profitability metrics offset by negative ROA, no dividends, cash flow concerns). Below average quality threshold.

## E. Philosophical Alignment — Howard Marks (5/15 pts)

**Assessment: The Quintessential "Unseemly" Opportunity — But Timing Is Everything**

> *"The best bargains are found among the unloved, the misunderstood, the 'unseemly.'"*

GOTO is perhaps the single most "unseemly" name on the IDX. An IPO that destroyed 84% of investor capital. A tech stock in a market that despises tech. A company that has never reported a GAAP profit. By every measure, the crowd has abandoned it — and Howard Marks would tell you this is precisely where to look for opportunity.

**But** — and this is critical second-level thinking — the contrarian case requires two conditions to be met:
1. **The intrinsic value must exceed the current price.** GOTO's adjusted EBITDA trajectory and fintech growth suggest intrinsic value is above IDR 55 — but how far above is uncertain given ongoing GAAP losses.
2. **The catalyst must be identifiable.** The Grab merger is a potential catalyst, but unconfirmed. The 2026F GAAP breakeven is a more reliable catalyst, but it's 12+ months away.

Howard would say: *"I'd want to own this in 6-12 months when the GAAP profit prints and the narrative shifts. Buying today is buying the thesis before the proof — and in a crisis market, the market doesn't reward early contrarians."*

**Verdict:** The philosophical alignment is strong but premature. The risk of permanent capital loss is moderate (unlike ACES, GOTO's moat is *growing*, not shrinking). But the lack of earnings, the hostile macro, and the leadership transition demand patience.
