## 🔥 BREAKING: Middle East Geopolitical Shock — Energy Focus

### Context
On **February 28, 2026**, a military conflict involving the US, Israel, and Iran escalated sharply, triggering what market participants describe as the most serious Gulf energy supply disruption since the 1970s oil embargo. The Strait of Hormuz — through which **20–25% of global seaborne crude and LNG** normally flows — has been **effectively paralysed**:

- Iranian threats and direct attacks on vessels have caused tanker operators to reroute or anchor, suspending Hormuz transits.
- Qatar's **Ras Laffan LNG facility** was shut down following an attack (reported March 2, 2026).
- **Saudi Arabia's Ras Tanura refinery** struck by aerial attacks — a key Gulf crude export terminal.
- Iraq signalling potential production cuts if shipping issues persist.
