### Institutional Crisis: The OJK/IDX Leadership Vacuum & Required Risk Premium

**What happened:**
On **January 28, 2026**, the MSCI transparency warning triggered a flash crash on the IHSG with automatic trading circuit breakers activated. Within 48 hours (Jan 30–31, 2026), **IDX President Director Iman Rachman resigned**, citing personal moral responsibility. Simultaneously, the **OJK Board Chair and multiple commissioners resigned** in a coordinated leadership exit framed as support for "market recovery." This is without precedent in Indonesian capital market history. The dual collapse of the exchange operator and the regulator's leadership compounded the crisis of confidence beyond the MSCI technical issue alone.

**Risk Premium Calculation — OJK/IDX Leadership Vacuum:**

The Equity Risk Premium (ERP) for a market reflects compensation for *all* risks incurred beyond the risk-free rate. The component-based breakdown for IHSG:

| Risk Component | Pre-Crisis Estimate | Crisis Adjustment | Post-Crisis Estimate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base EM Equity Risk Premium | +400 bps | +0 bps | +400 bps |
| Indonesia-specific country risk | +150 bps | +0 bps | +150 bps |
| Sovereign outlook downgrade premium (Moody's + Fitch) | +0 bps | **+75 bps** | +75 bps |
| MSCI Frontier reclassification option risk | +0 bps | **+100 bps** | +100 bps |
| OJK/IDX governance/leadership vacuum | +0 bps | **+50 bps** | +50 bps |
| **Total IHSG Equity Risk Premium** | **~550 bps** | **+225 bps added** | **~775 bps** |

> A 225 bps increase in required ERP is mechanically devastating to equity valuations. Using a Gordon Growth Model approximation: if IHSG earnings grew at 8% historically and the discount rate rises by 225 bps, the theoretical fair value of the index compresses by ~**20–30%** from pre-crisis levels. The IHSG is currently -17.8% from peak, meaning markets have priced in *roughly 80% of the theoretically warranted ERP adjustment*. The index is not yet in "full value" territory — but the worst of the indiscriminate selling may be approaching exhaustion.

**Why the leadership vacuum matters beyond optics:**
1. **Enforcement gap:** OJK is the sole regulator for market surveillance, margin lending oversight, and insider trading enforcement. A leadership vacuum during a crisis delays decisive policy responses.
2. **MSCI negotiation credibility:** MSCI cited governance and transparency concerns. The same regulators being replaced were the ones who would have led the reform dialogue. New leadership must rebuild MSCI trust from zero — taking months, not days.
3. **Foreign investor signaling:** International fund managers interpret leadership collapses as a signal of *deeper structural dysfunction*, not just a manageable market anomaly. It upgrades Indonesia from "cyclical EM risk" to "systemic EM governance risk" — a categorically worse investment designation.
4. **IDX demutualization commitment:** OJK promised to finalize IDX demutualization as part of reforms. The timeline is now uncertain under new leadership.
