## 🏦 Financials (IDXFIN) — `Equal-weight (Selective Deep Value)`

### MSCI-Triggered Rout: Liquidity Drain Analysis

The MSCI transparency warning (effective Feb 2026 rebalancing) froze all increases to Foreign Inclusion Factors (FIF) and banned upward segment migrations. This created a **forced selling cascade**: passive EM funds tracking MSCI indexes cannot add Indonesian holdings, and active funds preemptively reduced exposure ahead of a possible Frontier downgrade at the May 2026 MSCI review. Financials — the largest sector by weight in both the IHSG and MSCI Indonesia — absorbed the most indiscriminate selling.

**IDXFIN Price Action:** From ~1,484 (Jan 2026) to ~1,416 (Mar 5, 2026), a loss of ~4.6% in six weeks, underperforming its fundamental deterioration, which suggests **a meaningful component of the selloff is technical/flow-driven**, not fundamentals-based.

### Deep Value Assessment: Has Mispricing Emerged?

**Proxy Fundamental Analysis for Major Indonesian Banks (BBRI, BBCA, BMRI, BBNI):**

| Proxy Factor | Current Signal | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| **Dividend Yield vs. BI-Rate** | BBRI: ~8–9%, BBNI: ~6–7%, BMRI: ~5–6%. All yield above BI-Rate (4.75%) AND above 10Y bond (6.59%) in some cases | ✅ Income floor intact; positively mispriced vs. fixed income |
| **P/E Relative to Historical** | Major banks trading at 7–10x P/E vs. historical range of 10–15x | ✅ Historically cheap — flows-driven discount |
| **Piotroski F-Score Proxy** | Profitability (ROA positive ✅), Leverage (D/E stable ✅), Liquidity (CAR above BI minimum ✅), Efficiency (NIM stable ✅) — 4/4 proxy factors pass | ✅ Quality screen passes under stress |
| **Altman Z-Score Proxy** | For banks: adapted metric — NPL ratio rising but below systemic threshold; LDR manageable; CASA ratios high | ⚠️ Stress elevated but not distress territory |
| **Foreign Ownership Risk** | Foreign ownership in bank stocks ~15–25% — further MSCI-driven exit is plausible | ❌ Remain a source of additional downward pressure |
| **BI Rate Hold (No Cut)** | 5th consecutive hold — NIM not expanding; loan growth subdued | ❌ Negative — High cost of funds likely to persist |

**Verdict:** The MSCI-triggered selloff has created **genuine deep-value mispricing** in tier-1 Indonesian banks, primarily BBRI and BMRI, where dividend yields exceed the 10Y bond yield. However, re-rating requires a confirmed MSCI upgrade pathway (May 2026 review) and IDR stabilization. **Position: Selective accumulation at current levels with strict stop-losses.** This is not a broad IDXFIN overweight — quality discrimination is essential. Avoid smaller banks with higher NPL exposure and lower CASA ratios.

## 🏠 Property & Real Estate (IDXPROPERT) — `Underweight` (High Conviction)

### Sovereign Yield → Cost of Capital → Cap Rate Expansion

The Indonesia 10-Year bond yield at **6.59%** is the foundational risk-free rate from which all Indonesian real estate cap rates are priced. The mechanic:

> **Cap Rate = Risk-Free Rate + Illiquidity Premium + Asset Risk Premium**
> At 6.59% risk-free + 150–250 bps illiquidity + 100–200 bps asset risk = **implied cap rate of 9.09%–11.09%** for commercial property

When the 10Y yield rises by 25–50 bps due to downgrade pressure (which is the current trajectory), cap rates must expand by an equal or greater amount. This is **mechanically negative for asset valuations**: the same NOI (Net Operating Income) discounted at a higher cap rate produces a lower property value.

**Specific Asset Class Impact — Jakarta & Surabaya Primary Hubs:**

| Asset Class | Impact | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| **SOHO / Small Office-Home Office** | End-buyer financing via KPR/commercial mortgage at BI-Rate + 300–400 bps = **7.75–8.75% effective rate**. Already at multi-year demand lows. Downgrade premium adds 25–50 bps to bank funding cost = higher mortgage rates. | ❌ HIGH NEGATIVE |
| **Apartments (residential strata)** | KPR mortgage demand suppressed by high rates. Foreign buyer interest constrained by IDR weakness (16,914). | ❌ HIGH NEGATIVE |
| **Commercial Buildings (Grade A Office)** | Jakarta office vacancy already elevated post-COVID (~25–30%). Rising cap rates depress development IRR projections below developer hurdle rates. New supply pipeline at risk of deferral. | ❌ MODERATE-HIGH NEGATIVE |
| **Industrial / Logistics (secondary)** | Partially insulated — demand driven by domestic e-commerce and nearshoring. Less interest-rate sensitive. Slight relative outperformer within sector. | ⚠️ MODERATE NEGATIVE |

**Developer Balance Sheet Stress:** Multiple mid-tier developers carry USD-denominated notes. IDR at 16,914 increases effective debt service cost. **Verdict:** Risk of breach of fiscal 3% cap due to $112 oil creates a "wait and see" deadlock in property investment. Maintain **Underweight**.

## 🛒 Consumer Non-Cyclicals (Staples) — `Equal-weight (Bifurcated)`

### Makan Bergizi Gratis: Demand Jolt vs. IDR Margin Squeeze

The MBG program allocates **Rp 335 trillion** (~USD 20 billion) in 2026 to deliver daily nutritious meals to ~82.9 million beneficiaries (students, pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers). This creates a **substantial, direct government-backed revenue injection** for the food supply chain — spanning protein producers (poultry, eggs, beef), rice/grain distributors, dairy, and packaged food manufacturers.

**Demand Side (Positive):**
- Companies with MBG supply contracts receive *guaranteed volume* at government-set prices — a form of demand underwriting that bypasses consumer discretionary risk.
- Categories benefiting: eggs and poultry (JPFA, CPIN), infant formula and dairy (ULTJ, MYOR), packaged nutrition (ICBP, INDF).
- Koperasi Merah Putih (Rp 83T budget) channels spending through village cooperatives — supports local staple distribution companies.

**Cost Side (Negative — IDR Weakness):**
- Key raw materials for staples producers are imported or USD-linked: **wheat flour** (for instant noodles — ICBP, INDF), **soybean meal** (for poultry feed — JPFA, CPIN), **packaging materials**, **palm oil** (partially).
- With IDR at 16,914 vs. 200-day average of 16,535, COGS inflation from FX alone is **~2.3%** — before and layering on the **$112/bbl oil shock** which spikes logistics and distribution costs.
- Gross margin compression of 200–300 bps is the expected impact for imported-input-intensive producers.

**Net Assessment — Bifurcated View:**
- **Winners:** Domestic protein producers with MBG contracts and low import dependency (JPFA, especially if egg contracts confirmed). Volume growth offsets margin pressure.
- **Losers:** Processed food exporters and import-heavy manufacturers (ICBP/INDF flour-dependent products) where IDR COGS impact is direct and the MBG contract benefit is indirect/slower.
- **Verdict:** Equal-weight aggregate, but internal stock selection is critical. Prefer domestically-sourced protein names over wheat/import-dependent processors.

## ⛽ Energy & Commodities (IDXENERGY) — `Overweight` (High Conviction — Bifurcated)

Maintained from prior update for the broader sector, but with a **critical negative carve-out for Nickel**. WTI at **$112.00** (52w high + surge) provides massive tailwinds for coal exporters and oil service providers. IDXENERGY remains the **primary institutional hedge** against IDR weakness and global inflation. Major coal names (ITMG, PTBA, ADRO) with strong FCF and high dividend yields are the defensive sanctuary. **+5 pts fundamental bonus active per macro override rules.**

**⚠️ Critical Carve-out: Nickel / EV Battery Metals (UNDERWEIGHT)**
The narrative for Indonesian nickel has fundamentally fractured. The successful launch and rapid charging capabilities of BYD's Blade Battery 2.0 (LMFP/LFP chemistry) directly threatens the global market share of NMC (nickel-heavy) batteries. 
- **Structural Demand Destruction:** If fast-charging LFP dominates, the anticipated secular growth in Class 1 battery-grade nickel evaporates.
- **Stranded Asset Risk:** The multi-billion dollar HPAL smelter buildout in Indonesia, designed specifically for the NMC supply chain, risks becoming stranded capacity.
- **Valuation Disconnect:** Dominant producers like **INCO** trade at steep premiums (~55x P/E) to global peers like Nornickel (~15x P/E), despite facing a structural ceiling where nickel prices could stagnate in the $12,000-$15,000 range (driven solely by stainless steel/NPI demand). Avoid nickel plays heavily leveraged to the EV battery narrative.
