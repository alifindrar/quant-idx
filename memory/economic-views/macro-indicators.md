## 📌 Analytical Notes

### Note 1: BI-Rate vs. Indonesia 10Y Yield — The Downgrade Premium

> The Indonesia 10-Year bond yield sits at **6.59%**, a full **184 basis points (bps) above BI's policy rate of 4.75%**. A "normal" sovereign spread for a stable BBB-rated EM economy typically runs 100–140 bps over the policy rate. The excess of ~45–85 bps above expected represents a **downgrade risk premium** now embedded in Indonesian sovereign debt — a direct consequence of the consecutive Moody's (Feb 5, 2026) and Fitch (Mar 4, 2026) negative outlook moves.
>
> **Mechanism:** When both Moody's and Fitch shift outlooks to Negative, institutional bond holders with investment-grade mandates pre-emptively rotate out of Indonesian government bonds (SBN/SUN), driving yields higher (prices lower). Foreign ownership of Indonesian government bonds has historically been 15–22% of total issuance; even a 3–5% foreign exit implies tens of trillions in IDR selling pressure, which simultaneously weakens USD/IDR and pushes long-end yields up.
>
> **Critical Risk:** Fitch explicitly cited potential revision of the **State Finance Law** (which caps the deficit at 3% of GDP) as a trigger for an actual rating cut — not just an outlook change. If this ceiling is revised upward by the Prabowo administration, **Indonesia loses its primary sovereign fiscal anchor.** Bond yields would re-price sharply higher, likely toward 7.0–7.5%, which would materially compress bank NIM expansion opportunities and choke commercial real estate development pipelines.

### Note 2: Fiscal Deficit & the Rupiah's Structural Fragility

> **Confirmed 2026 Budget Deficit Target:** 2.68% of GDP (parliament-passed). However, Fitch's independent forecast is **2.9% of GDP** and OCBC projects **2.8%** — already at the edge of the 3% legal ceiling.
>
> **MBG allocations creating structural pressure:**
> - Makan Bergizi Gratis (MBG): **Rp 335 trillion** (~USD 20 billion) — largest single program allocation in the 2026 APBN.
> - Koperasi Merah Putih: **Rp 83 trillion** + Rp 34.57 trillion from village funds.
> - Combined welfare/populist spend: estimated **Rp 418 trillion+**, representing a *structural* (non-cyclical) expenditure that cannot easily be reversed without political cost.
>
> **Rupiah transmission path:** Elevated fiscal spending → wider deficit → higher SBN issuance to fund it → domestic bond supply exceeds private demand → foreign investors require yield premium → capital outflows → IDR depreciation. This is a self-reinforcing loop. The Rupiah at 16,881 vs. its 200-day MA of 16,535 reflects exactly this dynamic. The Finance Minister noted that **oil at $90–92/bbl could push the deficit to 3.6% of GDP** — and WTI is currently at $79.72 and rising, not far from that stress scenario. The government's contingency plans consist of unspecified "spending adjustments" — insufficient to calm rating agencies pre-emptively.
>
> **DXY Context:** The DXY at 98.98 is only modestly off its 52w high (-6.4%), meaning the IDR weakness is substantially domestic (policy credibility erosion), not merely USD-driven. A recovery in the Rupiah requires either: (a) BI raising rates (negative for growth/equities), (b) successful MSCI reform by May 2026 (uncertain), or (c) a sharp DXY weakening globally (not the base case given US economic resilience).

### Note 3: WTI Crude Oil at $112.00 — Indonesia's Fiscal Anchor SNAPS

> WTI Crude is at **$112.00**, having surged well beyond all major moving averages and the government's APBN 2026 oil price assumption ($82/bbl).
>
> **Fiscal Crisis Mechanism:** The Finance Minister previously flagged that **oil at $90–92/bbl could push the deficit to 3.6% of GDP**, breaching the 3% legal ceiling. At $112, the fuel subsidy burden (Pertalite, Solar, LPG) is unsustainable without either:
> 1. **Immediate Fuel Price Hikes:** (Highly unpopular, inflationary, would likely trigger social unrest/mass protests).
> 2. **Illegal Deficit Breaching:** (Would trigger immediate credit rating downgrade to Junk by all major agencies).
> 3. **Emergency Spending Reallocation:** (Siphoning funds from MBG or infrastructure, creating political friction).
>
> The current crude price represents a **systemic threat to Indonesia's investment-grade rating**. The Rupiah at 16,914 reflects the market's expectation of fiscal slippage or inflationary shocks.
