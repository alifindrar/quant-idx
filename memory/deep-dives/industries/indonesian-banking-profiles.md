# Indonesian Bank Profiles

> **Created:** 2026-03-11
> **Coverage:** 31 Listed Indonesian Banks
> **Parent Deep Dive:** [Indonesian Banking](indonesian-banking.md)

This document serves as a companion to the main Indonesian Banking primer, providing profiles for 31 specific publicly listed banks, categorizing their KBLI segment, economic moats, and differentiation strategies.

## KBLI Reference
- **64121 (Bank Umum Konvensional):** Conventional Commercial Banks.
- **64122 (Bank Umum Syariah):** Sharia Commercial Banks.

---

### Big 4 Banks

#### 1. BBCA - PT Bank Central Asia Tbk
- **Profile:** Indonesia's largest private bank and largest by market capitalization. Controlled by the Djarum Group, it is widely considered the gold standard for banking efficiency and transaction processing in Indonesia.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Low-Cost Deposit Franchise.** BBCA possesses an unassailable CASA (Current Account Savings Account) ratio due to its historically dominant ATM network, ubiquitous merchant EDC network, and best-in-class digital platforms (BCA Mobile, myBCA). This provides the cheapest funding base in the industry.
- **Differentiation:** Unmatched transactional banking infrastructure. It focuses on prime corporate/commercial borrowers and affluent consumers, prioritizing asset quality and low cost of funds over chasing risky high-yield lending.

#### 2. BBRI - PT Bank Rakyat Indonesia (Persero) Tbk
- **Profile:** The largest bank in Indonesia by assets, state-owned (SOE), with a historical mandate to serve the micro, small, and medium enterprise (MSME) segment.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Micro-Lending Distribution & Scale.** BBRI's moat is its massive physical presence (thousands of rural units) and its "Agen BRILink" network deeply penetrating remote areas. It has an effective monopoly in rural micro-lending where borrower price elasticity is low, giving it tremendous pricing power and high structural NIMs.
- **Differentiation:** Ultra-micro financial ecosystem (holding company integrating Pegadaian and PNM) that reaches the unbanked and underbanked at the bottom of the pyramid.

#### 3. BMRI - PT Bank Mandiri (Persero) Tbk
- **Profile:** State-owned bank resulting from the merger of four state banks during the Asian Financial Crisis. Historically a corporate banking powerhouse, it has successfully diversified into wholesale, commercial, and retail segments.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Corporate Banking Dominance & Ecosystem.** BMRI dominates wholesale banking and SOE value chains. It leverages its corporate relationships to capture payrolls and retail businesses of corporate employees.
- **Differentiation:** The "Kopra" (wholesale) and "Livin'" (retail) super-apps. BMRI has built the most successful digital transformation narrative among state-owned banks, seamlessly integrating its corporate dominance with a fast-growing retail digital ecosystem.

#### 4. BBNI - PT Bank Negara Indonesia (Persero) Tbk
- **Profile:** State-owned bank with a strong legacy in corporate lending, international business, and consumer segment (especially payroll-linked credit).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Institutional Relationships & Global Network.** BBNI holds strong relationships with top-tier corporates and has the most extensive overseas branch network among Indonesian banks, capturing trade finance and diaspora remittances.
- **Differentiation:** Focuses on serving the international trade needs of Indonesian businesses and capturing the value chain of prime corporates down to the consumer level (Tier-1 corporate focus). It has been structurally shifting to lower-risk assets to improve asset quality vs historical cycles.

### Mid-Tier & Major Ecosystem Banks

#### 5. BMAS - PT Bank Maspion Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Originally a bank serving the Maspion Group ecosystem in East Java, it is now majority-owned by Thailand's Kasikornbank (KBank).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Strategic Backing.** Kasikornbank's capital and technological prowess, combined with the legacy Maspion corporate network in East Java.
- **Differentiation:** A bridge for Thai-Indonesian trade and foreign direct investment, leveraging KBank's digital banking expertise to transform into a more digitally-capable MSME lender.

#### 6. BNGA - PT Bank CIMB Niaga Tbk
- **Profile:** A large private bank subsidiary of Malaysia's CIMB Group. Strong in corporate banking, mortgages, and auto financing (via its subsidiary Niaga Auto Finance).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **ASEAN Network & Affluent Consumer Base.** Capitalizes on cross-border corporate flows within ASEAN. It also maintains a sticky affluent consumer deposit base built over decades.
- **Differentiation:** Strong regional synergy with its parent company and a pioneer in Indonesian digital banking with "OCTO Mobile". It successfully runs a large Sharia banking unit (UUS) capturing Islamic finance growth.

#### 7. BNII - PT Bank Maybank Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Subsidiary of Malaysia's Malayan Banking Berhad (Maybank). Provides comprehensive corporate, commercial, and retail banking services.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Parent Company Support.** Backed by the scale, capital, and cross-border capabilities of the Maybank Group.
- **Differentiation:** Focus on ASEAN regional connectivity, specializing in supply chain financing and offering a comprehensive Sharia first strategy ("Sharia First") leveraging Maybank's Islamic banking leadership globally.

#### 8. BNLI - PT Bank Permata Tbk
- **Profile:** Formerly owned by Standard Chartered and Astra, acquired in 2020 by Bangkok Bank from Thailand.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Foreign Corporate Channeling.** Direct access to Bangkok Bank's vast Thai and regional corporate clients investing in Indonesia.
- **Differentiation:** Positioned as the bank of choice for foreign direct investment (particularly from Thailand and the broader Indochina region) while maintaining a solid domestic commercial and SME footprint.

#### 9. BRIS - PT Bank Syariah Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** The largest Islamic bank in Indonesia, formed by the merger of the Syariah units of BBRI, BMRI, and BBNI. State-owned.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64122 (Sharia Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Scale Monopoly in Islamic Finance.** As the only massive-scale Sharia bank in the world's most populous Muslim country, it benefits from government mandates (e.g., managing Hajj funds, state payrolls) and captive religious-conscious demographics. It has near-zero cost of funds from certain Sharia deposits (Wadi'ah).
- **Differentiation:** The undisputed champion of the Sharia economy in Indonesia. Cannot be replicated by conventional peers without enormous capital and structural spin-offs.

#### 10. BBTN - PT Bank Tabungan Negara (Persero) Tbk
- **Profile:** State-owned bank that dominates Indonesia's mortgage market, especially subsidized mortgages for lower-income segments.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Subsidized Mortgage Monopoly.** Holds overwhelming market share in the government's subsidized housing program (FLPP). Its deep integration with property developers and government schemes creates a nearly impenetrable niche.
- **Differentiation:** Highly specialized balance sheet, heavily weighted toward long-term mortgages, requiring it to constantly manage wholesale funding costs. 

#### 11. BDMN - PT Bank Danamon Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** A major bank now majority-owned by Japan's MUFG (Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group). Acquired Adira Finance, a massive automotive multi-finance company.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **MUFG Ecosystem & Adira.** Anchored by the immense balance sheet and Japanese corporate client base of MUFG, paired with the massive consumer captive audience of Adira Finance for auto loans.
- **Differentiation:** Forms the core of the "MUFG Indonesia Ecosystem" (which now includes Mandala Finance and Home Credit Indonesia). Strong dominance in auto financing value chains and Japanese corporate banking.

### Digital & Tech-Ecosystem Banks

#### 12. AGRO - PT Bank Raya Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Originally an agribusiness-focused bank, it has pivoted to become the digital attacker arm of its parent, BBRI.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **BBRI Ecosystem Access.** Can tap into the massive unbanked/underbanked pool of BBRI's ultra-micro ecosystem while sharing BBRI's infrastructure and data points.
- **Differentiation:** Functions as a gig-economy and digital-native lender for informal workers and small agents (e.g., Pinang platform), taking higher margin, smaller-ticket digital loans that are too granular or fast-moving for traditional BBRI processes.

#### 13. AMAR - PT Bank Amar Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Owned by the Tolaram Group, renowned for its flagship digital unsecured lending platform, "Tunaiku".
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Proprietary Credit Scoring.** "Tunaiku" was one of the earliest digital platforms in Indonesia to successfully algorithmically underwrite uncollateralized loans to the underbanked, leading to vast data accumulation.
- **Differentiation:** Pure-play high-yield, high-risk uncollateralized consumer digital lending. It operates much like a fintech P2P lender but with a banking license, allowing it to take retail deposits to fund its book.

#### 14. ARTO - PT Bank Jago Tbk
- **Profile:** A tech-based digital bank deeply integrated with the GOTO (Gojek Tokopedia) ecosystem. Backed by veteran banker Jerry Ng and GIC.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Platform Integration.** Embedded directly into GoPay and Tokopedia, allowing seamless, frictionless account opening and transactions for millions of active ecosystem users.
- **Differentiation:** Tech-native architecture combined with a "partnership model." Rather than building its own massive direct lending team, ARTO channels funds through multifinance, P2P, and ecosystem partners like GoTo Financial.

#### 15. BANK - PT Bank Aladin Syariah Tbk
- **Profile:** Indonesia's first pure-play digital Sharia bank. It has a strategic partnership with Alfamart, a massive convenience store chain.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64122 (Sharia Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **O2O (Online-to-Offline) Network.** By partnering with Alfamart, BANK effectively gains >15,000 physical touchpoints for cash-in/cash-out and customer acquisition without the CapEx of building branches.
- **Differentiation:** Combines Islamic finance principles with modern digital banking and deep offline retail integration (Alfamart ecosystem).

#### 16. BBHI - PT Allo Bank Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** The digital bank of the CT Corp conglomerate (Chairul Tanjung), transitioning from a small legacy bank (Bank Harda) to a digital ecosystem orchestrator. Includes backing from Bukalapak, Salim, and Grab.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **CT Corp Captive Market.** Exclusive access to CT Corp's retail empire (Transmart, Wendy's, Coffee Bean, Trans Studio) and media networks, giving immediate, subsidized customer acquisition channels.
- **Differentiation:** An "ecosystem within an ecosystem"—aggregating benefits, point redemptions, and BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) across the offline retail footprint of CT Corp.

#### 17. BBSI - PT Krom Bank Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Formerly Bank Bisnis Internasional, acquired by FinAccel (parent of Kredivo, a massive BNPL platform) and rebranded as Krom Bank.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Kredivo Customer Base.** Direct pipeline to Kredivo's millions of approved credit users, migrating them to full banking products while utilizing Kredivo's proven credit scoring models.
- **Differentiation:** Designed to provide cheap retail deposit funding (to replace expensive wholesale borrowing) to fund the high-yield Kredivo BNPL loan book.

#### 18. BBYB - PT Bank Neo Commerce Tbk
- **Profile:** Formerly Bank Yudha Bhakti, transformed into a digital bank backed by Akulaku (a major Indonesian ecommerce and BNPL platform backed by Ant Group).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Akulaku Origination Engine.** Derives vast majority of its consumer loans directly channeled from Akulaku's platform, guaranteeing high volume.
- **Differentiation:** Rapid aggressive deposit gathering via high-yield digital promos ("Neobank" app) to feed Akulaku's massive, high-yield consumer loan engine.

#### 19. BINA - PT Bank Ina Perdana Tbk
- **Profile:** Controlled by the Salim Group (Indofood, Indomaret).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Salim Group Ecosystem.** Potential to tap into the sprawling Indomaret convenience store network and Indofood supply chains across the archipelago.
- **Differentiation:** Still acting largely as a traditional commercial bank, but its differentiation lies in its captive access to the supply chain financing needs of Indonesia's largest consumer goods conglomerate.

### Regional & Niche Banks

#### 20. BJBR - PT Bank Pembangunan Daerah Jawa Barat dan Banten Tbk (Bank BJB)
- **Profile:** The regional development bank (BPD) for West Java and Banten, regions with massive economic activity and population.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Captive Civil Servant Market.** Near-monopoly on the payrolls of local government civil servants (PNS), municipal deposits, and regional infrastructure projects.
- **Differentiation:** Highly sticky, extremely low-risk consumer loan book consisting of salary-deducted loans for civil servants and retirees against captive payrolls.

#### 21. BJTM - PT Bank Pembangunan Daerah Jawa Timur Tbk (Bank Jatim)
- **Profile:** The regional development bank for East Java.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Captive Regional Government Revenue.** Similar to BJBR, but specifically holding the monopoly on East Java provincial and regency funds and civil servant payrolls.
- **Differentiation:** Exceptional dividend yield derived from stable, low-risk, albeit slow-growth, captive captive civil-servant salary lending and regional treasury management.

#### 22. BBMD - PT Bank Mestika Dharma Tbk
- **Profile:** A regional private bank deeply anchored in North Sumatra.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Regional Relationship Density.** Deep, multi-generational relationships with local traders, palm oil businesses, and SMEs in the North Sumatran economy.
- **Differentiation:** Hyper-localized commercial banking. Consistently operates with very high capital ratios and conservative lending, surviving crises by intimately knowing its local borrower base.

#### 23. BGTG - PT Bank Ganesha Tbk
- **Profile:** A smaller commercial bank backed by Equity Development Investment (part of the Equity Group).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **None / Weak.** Relies largely on shareholder ecosystem (insurance and multifinance links) but lacks systemic structural advantages.
- **Differentiation:** Niche commercial and SME lending, often functioning as a channel for P2P fintechs to deploy funds, seeking to transition into a more digitally-capable niche player.

#### 24. BEKS - PT Bank Pembangunan Daerah Banten Tbk
- **Profile:** The regional development bank specifically for the Banten province, spun off and struggling with historical asset quality issues.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Provincial Support.** Backed by the Banten provincial government, giving it access to local state-owned enterprise business and regional treasuries.
- **Differentiation:** Turnaround story reliant completely on the Banten government's commitment to route provincial civil servant payrolls and budgets through the bank.

#### 25. BNBA - PT Bank Bumi Arta Tbk
- **Profile:** A legacy small private bank that received investment from Ajaib (a massive retail stockbroking and crypto platform).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Ajaib Integration Potential.** The primary moat relies on upcoming integration with Ajaib's millions of retail investors for clearing, RDN (investor accounts), and margin financing.
- **Differentiation:** Shifting from a traditional SME lender to becoming the banking backbone of one of Indonesia's largest retail investment fintech platforms.

### Foreign-Acquired & Restructuring Banks

#### 26. AGRS - PT Bank IBK Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Formed from the merger of Bank Agris and Bank Mitraniaga, acquired by Industrial Bank of Korea (IBK).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Korean FDI Channeling.** Serves as the primary financial conduit for the vast number of South Korean manufacturing and corporate entities operating in Indonesia.
- **Differentiation:** Niche focus on Korean-Indonesian trade and enterprise supply chains, bringing IBK's global SME rating expertise to the local market.

#### 27. BBKP - PT KB Bank Tbk
- **Profile:** Formerly Bank Bukopin, saved from distress and fully acquired by South Korea's KB Kookmin Bank.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Sovereign-Level Backing.** KB Financial Group (Korea) has poured massive capital and technological resources into restructuring the bank.
- **Differentiation:** Massive, ongoing multi-year turnaround attempting to leverage KB Kookmin's advanced IT architecture (Next Generation IT) and capital strength to clean up legacy bad loans and restart growth in commercial/SME segments.

#### 28. BCIC - PT Bank JTrust Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Formerly the infamous Bank Century (later Bank Mutiara), acquired and rehabilitated by Japan's J Trust Co., Ltd.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Japanese Retail & SME Network.** Leverages J Trust's global expertise in consumer finance and links to Japanese mid-market enterprises.
- **Differentiation:** Distinct focus on heavy consumer financing, multi-finance partnerships, and aggressively expanding property/mortgage lending via tie-ups with Japanese developers in Indonesia.

#### 29. BACA - PT Bank Capital Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Small to mid-sized commercial bank historically focused on corporate and SME lending.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **None / Weak.** Operates in a highly competitive space without a distinct digital parent or monopolistic advantage.
- **Differentiation:** Maintains boutique relationships with specific corporate groups and relies on partnership lending with fintechs/multifunds to expand reach efficiently.

#### 30. BKSW - PT Bank QNB Indonesia Tbk
- **Profile:** Subsidiary of Qatar National Bank (QNB), the largest financial institution in the Middle East and Africa.
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **Parent Capital & Wholesale Capacity.** QNB's immense balance sheet enables BKSW to participate in massive syndicated loans and trade finance that small local banks cannot.
- **Differentiation:** Elite corporate and wholesale banking focus, acting as the gateway for Middle Eastern capital deployment into Indonesian infrastructure and blue-chip corporates.

#### 31. BABP - PT Bank MNC Internasional Tbk
- **Profile:** The banking arm of Hary Tanoesoedibjo's MNC Group (media and financial property conglomerate).
- **KBLI Segment:** 64121 (Conventional Commercial Bank)
- **Economic Moat:** **MNC Media Ecosystem.** Capable of leveraging MNC's massive free-to-air TV reach and pay-TV subscriber base for customer acquisition.
- **Differentiation:** Operates "MotionBanking," part of the broader Motion ecosystem (MNC's digital financial services). Attempts to cross-sell banking services to MNC's vast media and entertainment consumer base.
